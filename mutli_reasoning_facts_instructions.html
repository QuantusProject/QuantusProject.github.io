FAQ

  1. Can the information from the URL link of text snippet be used in the question?

    Information from the link provided can be used in the question only if the fact has that information fully contained. If information is missing then please add fact with full information and select that fact in your annotation.

    e.g.

    FACT: To that end, during his years on the farm and immediately after World War I, he became active in several business ventures, including a lead and zinc mine near Commerce, Oklahoma.

    Link: Harry S. Truman - Wikipedia

    Explain:The fact is not complete, it does not mention who "he" is - hence add new fact with complete information explicitly and his name 'Harry Truman' and then use it in annotation.

  2. How do I copy any content from my fact to do further research? The highlighting feature interferes with that?

    Please use the highlight feature - once you do you will see a copy icon on left of highlighted text to quickly copy and lookup and do further research if needed.

  3. Does that information count when determining if the question can be answered by only one of the facts?

    Ideally, we want to find questions that need reasoning over multiple facts - each fact providing distinct information. If your question can be answered completely just from one fact then it is not a valid question.

What is this HIT about?

We are trying to build a question-answering system which is able to retrieve the answer by reasoning over multiple facts. This is the way we human typically use to find an answer. For example, when this question is asked:

Has there always been a rivalry between Daffy Duck and Porky Pig?

We might find the answer by

  1. Searching Wikipedia for Daffy Duck.
  2. "Daffy first appeared in Porky's Duck Hunt, released on April 17, 1937."
  3. "Porky shoots the whiskey-filled barrel but Daffy escapes" or "Daffy bites Porky on the nose"
  4. Conclude, Yes, Daffy and Porky have been rivals since their first appearance on screen.

However, for now, if you search a question in a search engine like Google or Bing, you are mostly like to get the answer from only one snippet of text. They don’t have the ability to answer the above question because, as you can see, the answer is concluded by reasoning the facts from multiple resources. We believe that this reasoning ability can greatly improve the question-answering ability of search engines, so we create this Hitapp and asking help from you. Appreciate!

The Format:

Each hit starts with a group of a minimum of 4 text snippets provided by us. These are somehow related to each other by some common topics or common entities. You may use these 4 to formulate your questions and are encouraged to add more related text snippets if those are needed to formulate your question.

A valid annotation should include:

  1. Select two or more text snippets fom the given group .This should include any new facts that you may have added.
  2. For your convenience you can use the highlighting feature we provide to highlight part of text/keywords in given facts. These should help you to organize, capture and refer back main thoughts while formulating a multi-hop reasoning question.
  3. Ask a question about the text snippets you select. To get the answer to question must need distinct info from multiple facts.
  4. Write down the correct answer of your query. The answer should be concluded from ALL text snippets you selected.
  5. The question should Not be a Yes/No question or comparision question.

Adding facts:

Feel free to add new fact if the given facts are not enough to format new question and answer. Follow the following instruction

  1. The new fact should be somehow related to the given one;
  2. The new fact should be complicated enough, and the surrending text should also be included.
  3. All new fact should be extract from webpage, please also provide the URL to the webpage in the given field.
  4. Plase DO NOT make up any fact by yourself.

Some other requests:

  1. Shouldn’t be answered by only one fact:

    Facts:

    1. Hawa was born on December 16th, 1937 in Umarwas.
    2. Hawa enrolled in the Indian Army in 1956, and became the champion of the Western Command in 1960 by defeating the defending champion, Mohabbat Singh.

    Bad question:When is Hawa’s birthday.(can be answer by the first fact)

    Good question: How old was Hawa when he enrolled in the Indian Army? (need both facts)

  2. Has definitive answer: The answer to your query cannot be too subjective or change from time to time.
    • How good is the view of Chicage from Willis Tower skydeck? (Too subjective)
    • What is the weather in Manhattan? (Time sensitive)
  3. Not about simple facts: The query cannot be answered blindly based on common sense (i.e. without using any of the text snippets).
    • Can a boat float on the water?
    • How many wheels does a car have?

Examples:

Example 1:

Facts:

  1. "Blue Moon of Kentucky" is a waltz written in 1945 by bluegrass musician Bill Monroe.
  2. The Bluegrass Album was released on September 24, 2013 by Alan Jackson. Jackson wrote eight songs for the album including "Blue Moon of Kentucky".
  3. The song was revered at the Grand Ole Opry and others; Carl Perkins played an uptempo version of this song in his early live performances.
  4. Bill Monroe's well-known song "Blue Moon of Kentucky" has been covered not only by bluegrass butalso rock and country artists, most notably Elvis Presley, Paul McCartney, and Patsy Cline.
  5. In 2002, Monroe's version was one of 50 recordings chosen that year by the Library of Congress to be added to the National Recording Registry.

Selected facts: 1,2,4

Question: Which bluegrass classic by Bill Monroe was covered by Elvis Presley, and also on Alan Jackson's 2013 Bluegrass Album?

Answer: "Blue Moon of Kentucky" the bluegrass classic was covered Elvis Presley, and also on Alan Jackson's 2013 Bluegrass Album.
